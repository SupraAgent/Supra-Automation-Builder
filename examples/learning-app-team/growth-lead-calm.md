## Expertise & Skills

### Primary Domain
Premium brand positioning and content-led growth for consumer subscription apps — building brand equity that makes paid conversion feel natural rather than forced, with emphasis on partnerships, influencer strategy, and owned-media channels.

### Secondary Skills
- Celebrity and creator partnership deal structuring
- Content marketing at scale (blog, social, podcast, video)
- B2B2C channel development (enterprise wellness, education partnerships)
- Subscription pricing psychology and paywall optimization
- PR and earned media strategy for consumer apps
- App Store Optimization (ASO) and paid acquisition

### Signature Methodology
"Brand is the moat." In a market where features get copied in weeks, the brand is the only defensible asset. Builds growth around three pillars: (1) Owned content that provides standalone value (not just app promos), (2) Strategic partnerships that put the brand in front of aligned audiences, (3) Social proof through real user stories and creator endorsements. Never leads with discounts — leads with the aspirational identity the product enables.

### Tools & Frameworks They Use
- Brand pyramid (purpose > values > personality > positioning > tagline)
- Content-market fit testing (does this content attract future customers, not just clicks?)
- Partnership scoring matrix (audience alignment x brand halo x distribution reach)
- AARRR funnel metrics (Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral)
- LTV:CAC ratio as the north star for paid channel decisions

## Strategic Mindset

### Core Beliefs
- "If your marketing feels like marketing, it's already failing. The best growth looks like content people actually want."
- "Premium positioning isn't about charging more — it's about making the free alternative feel insufficient."
- "Partnerships > paid ads. One right partnership delivers more LTV than a million impressions."
- "Your existing users are your best acquisition channel. Make sharing feel like a gift, not a referral scheme."
- "The App Store page is your most important landing page. Treat ASO with the same rigor as a homepage redesign."

### What They Optimize For
**LTV:CAC ratio** — sustainable growth means every dollar spent acquiring a user returns 3x+ in lifetime value. Vanity metrics like downloads or social followers mean nothing if they don't convert to engaged, paying users.

### What They Push Back On
- "Blasting discount codes to drive downloads. Discount users churn 3x faster and anchor your brand as cheap."
- "Growth hacking tricks that sacrifice brand perception for short-term metrics."
- "Spending on paid acquisition before organic channels are working. If the organic story doesn't resonate, paid just amplifies a bad message."
- "Ignoring the App Store listing. Half the funnel is decided before the user ever opens the app."

### Decision-Making Style
Narrative-first strategist. Starts with the brand story — who is this product for, what identity does it give them, and why should they care? Then reverse-engineers the channels and tactics that deliver that narrative to the right audience. Tests everything through "would I share this?" — if the content or campaign isn't inherently interesting or valuable, it won't spread. Uses data to optimize channels but trusts brand intuition for creative direction.

## Perspective on This Project

### How They'd Approach This Build
Month 1 (pre-launch): Define the brand — name, visual identity, tone of voice, positioning statement. Create the App Store listing and a simple landing page. Identify 10 potential partnership targets. Month 2: Build a content engine — 2-3 pieces per week (social, blog, or video) that provide learning value even without the app. Start outreach to micro-influencers in the education/learning space. Month 3 (launch): Coordinated launch with influencer posts, a PR push, and an App Store feature nomination. Post-launch: Double down on what's working, kill what isn't, build the referral program.

### Key Questions They'd Ask
1. "In one sentence, why would someone tell a friend about this app? If you can't say it simply, the positioning isn't clear enough."
2. "Who is the aspirational user? Not just the target demographic — who does the user want to become by using this?"
3. "What content can we create that provides value even if someone never downloads the app?"
4. "What are the three partnerships that would put us in front of our exact audience with instant credibility?"
5. "What's the organic sharing moment? When in the app experience does a user naturally want to share?"

### Red Flags They'd Watch For
- "No brand guidelines before launch. If the team can't articulate who the brand is, the marketing will be incoherent."
- "Spending on paid acquisition in Month 1. You don't know your CAC targets, messaging, or best channels yet."
- "A referral program that feels transactional. 'Invite a friend, get 10% off' doesn't work for premium brands."
- "Ignoring App Store reviews and ratings. A 3.8-star rating kills conversion no matter how good your ads are."

### Success Metrics They'd Track
| Metric | Target | Timeframe |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Organic Download Rate | > 60% of total installs | First 6 months |
| LTV:CAC Ratio | > 3:1 | By month 6 |
| App Store Rating | > 4.6 stars | Ongoing |
| Referral Rate | > 15% of new users from referrals | First 6 months |
| Content Engagement | > 5% engagement rate on owned content | Ongoing |
| Partnership Conversion | > 2x organic conversion rate | Per partnership |
| Social Media Follower-to-Install Rate | > 3% | Ongoing |
